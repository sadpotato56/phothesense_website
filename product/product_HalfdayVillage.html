Journey into the Soul of Vietnam's Crafts

A full-day sensory adventure connecting you with the masters of Incense, Conical Hats, and Lacquer Art.

About this journey

  • Duration: Full Day (approx. 8 hours)
  • Locations: Chuong Village, Quang Phu Cau, Ha Thai Village
  • Hands-on: Make your own Hat & Lacquer art
  • Photography: Award-winning incense fields
  • Meal: Authentic home-cooked lunch at an Artisan's House
  • Free cancellation up to 24 hours in advance
1. The Bridge to the Spirit World (Quang Phu Cau)

Your journey begins where the air is scented with cinnamon and the ground blooms in vibrant pink. At Quang Phu Cau, you won't just see incense; you'll understand its sacred role as a bridge to the ancestors. Walk through the drying fields that won Gold Awards in travel photography and capture your own masterpiece in traditional costume. Witness the mesmerizing rhythm of bamboo splitting and dyeing—a craft that nourishes the Vietnamese soul.

2. The Royal Covering (Chuong Village)

Next, we welcome you into a local Artisan House at Chuong Village. Here, the humble Nón Lá is more than a hat; it’s a feat of engineering. You’ll touch the dried palm leaves, witnessing their transformation from green to ghost-white.

Under the guidance of master artisans, try your hand at the most delicate steps: ironing the crinkled leaves with a traditional cloth ball and stitching them onto 16 perfect bamboo rings. It takes 13 hours to sew a single hat—can you master the first stitch?

3. "Better Weird Than Boring" (Ha Thai Village)

Conclude your day at Ha Thai Lacquer Village with an exclusive workshop led by an Outstanding Artist. Discover why lacquer painting is one of Vietnam's most expensive and time-consuming art forms. Imbued with the philosophy of "better weird than boring," you will create your own unique piece of lacquer art to take home—a personal fragment of Vietnam’s artistic heritage.
